Slide 1 – AI for agencies: save time, grow revenue

Imagine managing 40, maybe 400 WordPress sites. It’s Monday morning. Two red alerts hit your dashboard and a new client signed last night. How do you keep up? With Airo at GoDaddy, you handle it in minutes.

Slide 2 – Automate what drains your time

Here’s the frame for today: focus on automating the work that eats up your time, and save your judgment for where it matters most.

Slide 3 – The promise for the next 10 minutes

First we’ll tame the red alerts, then draft the new client’s site, and finally show how GoDaddy backs your growth. Along the way you’ll collect three rules any agency can use when pairing AI with human judgment.

Slide 4 – Monday morning at your agency

Before we dive in, a quick pulse-check. Who here manages more than 10 sites? 50? 100? Perfect—what you’re about to see is built for exactly that scale.

Slide 5 – Your control tower

Here’s what the Hub makes possible: one place to keep tabs on every site no matter where they’re hosted, see issues before they escalate, and standardize the fixes. When you can pull every alert into a single view and apply updates in batches, your team’s time moves from firefighting to advising clients—which is exactly where AI should support us. That’s our first Rule: AI owns the repetitive work.

Slide 6 – Hub agent in action

Let’s start with those red alerts.

With Hub Agent I type: “Show me all websites with alerts.” In moments I see exactly where to act.

Next: “Update plugins for all critical alerts.” Hours of work—done in minutes. That’s Rule 1 in action: AI owns the repetitive work.

I can also type: “Run a security scan on [SITE]” The agent executes, no dashboard digging.

Here’s the outcome: instead of logging into 20 dashboards, I batch tasks, save hours, and keep clients informed.

Slide 7 – Break the content bottleneck

Now changing gears to address that new client. Content is still the number-one blocker—83.5% of agencies say so. Airo Site Designer cracks that bottleneck by turning raw discovery notes into structure, copy, and Gutenberg blocks in minutes. While the agents automate the busywork, your team stays on the strategic, client-facing tasks. That’s Rule 2: AI drafts, but humans keep judgment where it matters. Let me show you how that plays out in the interface.

Slide 8 – Design with prompts

I’ll paste my discovery notes into the chat.

In seconds Airo generates a structured site brief. After confirming everything looks good, our specialized website agents do the rest—automating the repetitive setup and resolving the content bottlenecks that usually slow projects down. The draft site is created in minutes using core Gutenberg blocks.

Now I'm not done. Maybe I want to adjust it a bit more before getting into WordPress. I’ll type: “Make the hero headline Find your flow in Denver.” Then: “Add a page for at-home yoga tips.” I’m not clicking through menus. I’m telling Airo the outcome I'm looking for and it’s able to accomplish it.

With this draft ready, I can continue to WordPress where it’s my time to shine.

That’s Rule 2: let AI draft, but keep your judgment where it matters. You and your team finish layouts, plugins, and content—staying fully in WordPress.

Now we’ve managed the fire drills and impressed the new client. But what about keeping your pipeline full and making sure you’re not on your own when problems pop up?

You win the lead. You keep your weekend. And this is how the pieces connect: with Hub Agent you handled alerts, with Site Designer you built the draft, and through the Partner Program you grow.

Slide 10 – One workflow, three wins

Hub Agent resolves the alerts, Site Designer builds the draft, and the Partner Program fuels the win. That’s the end-to-end story.

Slide 11 – Specialized agents that fill the gaps

Beyond those core moves, Airo has specialists—blog creator, logo generator, market research, site optimizer and many more. When you nail a workflow, turn it into a playbook so the whole team hits repeat.

Slide 12 – Audit your week

These fill in the gaps between managing sites, building drafts, and scaling your agency. When you find the AI workflows that work either using the agents within Airo or elsewhere, make it repeatable. And this is the third rule—turn repeatable workflows into playbooks so your whole team benefits.

The playbook is the guide your agency can rely on. Document the workflows so that others in your agency can immediately start reaping the benefits. And inside that playbook sits your AI operating model: what tasks AI owns, what humans own, and how you measure the time you save. The operating model is one chapter, the playbook is the whole book.

Here’s one action item to make it real. Run a quick audit this week. Write down your daily and weekly tasks. Circle the ones that repeat again and again—those are strong candidates for AI. Then mark the ones where judgment, creativity, or relationships matter most. Those stay with you and your team. This simple audit shows you where AI fits and where your human edge remains critical.

The three rules are simple: AI owns the repetitive work, humans keep the judgment, and playbooks scale your wins. You just saw each one in action.
